<Section icon={Target} title="Offensive Execution: Passing and Catching Mechanics">

Offense in NFL Universe Football is a game of timing and "reads." As a Quarterback (QB), your primary goal is to identify open receivers and deliver the ball with the correct velocity. The passing system is not a simple click-to-aim mechanic; it involves a hold-and-release system that determines the arc and speed of the football.

Receivers, on the other hand, must master the art of the catch. Simply standing under the ball is rarely enough against skilled Defensive Backs. You must time your catch input to secure the ball at its highest point or dive to grab a low-thrown bullet pass.

**How to Execute a Perfect Passing Play:**

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Survey the Field">
    Before the snap, look at the defensive alignment. Identify which receivers are likely to have 1-on-1 coverage.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Snap and Drop Back">
    Once the ball is snapped, use WASD to drop back into the pocket. Avoid running backward too far, as this makes the throw more difficult.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Charge Your Throw">
    Hold the Left Click (PC) or the Throw Button (Mobile). A longer hold increases power for deep "hail mary" passes, while a quick tap is for short slants.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Lead the Receiver">
    Release the throw toward where the receiver is going, not where they currently are. This is called "leading" and is essential for avoiding interceptions.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Offensive Action | PC Input | Mobile Input | Success Key |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Throw Pass** | Hold Left Click | Hold Throw Button | Release based on receiver distance |
| **Catch Ball** | Left Click (Timed) | Catch Button | Time the press as the ball arrives |
| **Handoff** | X | Handoff Button | Requires proximity to the RB |
| **Pump Fake** | Double Click | Double Tap | Use to draw defenders out of position |
| **Block** | X | Block Button | Essential for protecting the QB |

<Callout type="warning" title="Quarterback Warning">
Holding the throw button for too long will max out the power, often resulting in an overthrow. Practice the "sweet spot" for mid-range passes in the Practice Mode to ensure your receivers don't have to jump unnecessarily.
</Callout>

<Section icon={Shield} title="Defensive Dominance: Tackling and Coverage Inputs">

Defense is often considered the hardest part of the game because it requires reacting to the opponent's movements. To be an effective defender, you must master the **NFL Universe Football controls** for diving and swatting. The "E" key on PC is your most powerful tool, allowing you to launch your player forward for a game-saving tackle or a spectacular interception.

Effective defense is split into two categories: Man Coverage (following a specific player) and Zone Coverage (guarding a specific area). Regardless of the strategy, your mechanical execution must be flawless.
