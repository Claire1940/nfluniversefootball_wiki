<Section icon={Target} title="Understanding the Trucking Mechanic">

Trucking in NFL Universe Football is more than just a decorative animation; it is a physics-based interaction between the ball carrier and the defender. Formerly known as Ultimate Football, this rebranded experience by Voldex utilizes a complex OVR (Overall Rating) system where your player's physical stats directly determine the outcome of a collision. When you initiate a truck, the game calculates your **Momentum**, **Strength**, and **Trucking OVR** against the defender's **Tackle** and **Size** stats.

Success in trucking allows you to maintain your forward progress, often leaving the defender on the turf. However, a failed truck can result in a fumble or a significant loss of yards. Understanding the risk-to-reward ratio is the first step toward becoming an elite power runner in the 2026 meta.

| Move Type | Primary Input | Best Use Case | Stamina Cost | Risk Level |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Trucking** | **F Key / Power Button** | Powering through 1v1 tackles | **High** | Medium |
| **Juking** | **Q or E Keys** | Avoiding contact in open space | Medium | Low |
| **Stiff Arm** | **R Key** | Pushing defenders toward sideline | Low | Low |
| **Diving** | **Space / Dive Button** | Gaining final yard for First Down | Medium | **High** |

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Tip">
Always check your OVR before attempting to truck high-level defenders. If your Strength is significantly lower than the defender's Tackle stat, you are more likely to be stopped in your tracks or forced into a fumble.

<Section icon={Swords} title="How to Execute a Perfect Truck">

Executing the move correctly is a matter of timing and positioning. Many beginners make the mistake of "spamming" the action key, which leads to poor animations and wasted stamina. To master **NFL Universe Football how to truck**, you must wait until the defender is within a specific "strike zone"—roughly 2 to 3 yards in front of your player model.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Build Momentum">
    Engage the **Shift key** to sprint. A truck requires forward velocity to be effective. Attempting to truck from a standing position or while moving laterally will almost always fail.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Identify the Defender">
    Target a single defender. Trucking is less effective against "group tackles" where two or more defenders are closing in. Aim for the defender's center mass.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Time the Input">
    Press the **F key** (or the Power Move button on mobile) just as the defender begins their tackle animation. If you press it too early, you lose momentum; too late, and the tackle is already registered.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Follow Through">
    After the contact, continue holding the **Forward (W)** and **Sprint (Shift)** keys. If the truck is successful, your player will stumble forward or run over the defender, allowing for extra yards after contact.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Stamina Warning">
Trucking consumes a large chunk of your stamina bar. If your stamina is in the "red" zone, your truck animation will be slower and significantly weaker, making you an easy target for interceptions or forced fumbles.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Map} title="Tactical Situations: When to Truck">

Knowing *when* to truck is just as important as knowing *how*. In 6v6 matches or the competitive Ranked Park mode, defenders are often looking for the big hit. You can use their aggression against them by utilizing the truck move at the right moment.

**Best Situations for Trucking:**
- **One-on-One with a Kicker/Punter**: These roles usually have the lowest tackle stats.
- **Near the First Down Marker**: When you only need one yard, a truck can provide the necessary forward lean.
- **Against High-Speed/Low-Weight DBs**: Fast defenders often sacrifice Strength for Speed, making them prime targets for a power run.

**When to Avoid Trucking:**
- **Against Linebackers (LBs)**: These players are built to stop power runs and will often win the Strength check.
- **When Outnumbered**: If a second defender is nearby, they will likely tackle you while you are recovering from the first truck animation.
- **In the Open Field**: If you have green grass ahead, a juke is often safer and preserves more stamina for the sprint to the end zone.

| Defender Position | Trucking Success Rate | Recommended Action |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Defensive Back** | **High (80%)** | Truck directly through them |
| **Linebacker** | Medium (45%) | Use a Juke or Stiff Arm instead |
| **Defensive Lineman** | **Low (20%)** | Avoid contact entirely |
| **Kicker** | **Very High (95%)** | Power through with confidence |
