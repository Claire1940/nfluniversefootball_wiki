<Section icon={Shield} title="Team Playbook & 6v6 Coordination">

In 6v6 matches, unstructured play styles will quickly ruin your record. Successful teams operate with structured playbooks, clear division of labor, and disciplined defensive assignments. On defense, running around randomly is a guaranteed way to concede easy touchdowns.

Your defensive backfield must master zone coverage concepts. Man-to-man coverage is highly vulnerable to double moves and speed mismatches, whereas a disciplined Cover 2 or Cover 3 scheme limits big plays and forces the opposing quarterback to make risky, contested throws into tight windows.

| Offensive Play Style | Best Defensive Counter | Defensive Key Action | Risk Level |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Deep Hail Mary** | Cover 3 Zone | DBs drop deep, play the ball | **Low Risk** |
| **Short Slant Routes** | Cover 2 Under | Linebackers jump the inside passing lanes | **Medium Risk** |
| **Outside Running Plays** | Edge Containment | Defensive ends set the edge, force runner inside | **Low Risk** |
| **Quarterback Scramble** | QB Spy | Assign one defender to shadow the QB | **High Risk** |

<Callout type="info" title="Play Calling Tempo">
Use the play-calling interface efficiently. Rushing your selection can lead to misaligned formations, while taking too long gives the defense extra time to read your personnel adjustments.
</Callout>
