<Section icon={Swords} title="Mastering Controls and Gameplay Mechanics">

The difference between a dropped ball and a game-winning catch in **NFL Universe Football** often comes down to milliseconds. Mastering the control scheme is the first step toward mechanical mastery. The game supports a variety of inputs, and knowing when to use specific modifiers like the Sprint or Dive can flip the momentum of a drive.

On PC, the keyboard offers precision for passing and tackling, while mobile players benefit from intuitive touch buttons for quick reactions. Below is the essential control breakdown for all aspiring legends.

| Action | PC Input | Mobile Input | Strategic Use Case |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sprint** | Shift Key | Sprint Button | Creating separation or closing gaps on defense |
| **Throw Pass** | Hold/Release Left Click | Tap/Hold Throw | Adjusting power for deep bombs or short bullets |
| **Catch** | Left Click (Timed) | Catch Button | Essential for WRs and Interceptions for DBs |
| **Dive/Tackle** | E Key | Dive Button | Stopping a runner or making a clutch goal-line stand |
| **Jump** | Spacebar | Jump Button | Contesting high balls in the end zone |
| **Block** | X Key | Block Button | Protecting your QB or clearing a lane for the RB |

<Callout type="warning" title="Timing is Everything">
Avoid "spamming" the catch or tackle buttons. NFL Universe Football uses a timing window; pressing the button too early often results in a failed animation, leaving you out of position and vulnerable to a big play.
</Callout>
