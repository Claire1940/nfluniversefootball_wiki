<Section icon={Swords} title="Competitive Position Strategies">

To climb the **NFL Universe Football leaderboard**, you cannot be a "jack of all trades." Specialization is the key to high-level Park play. Whether you are representing the Kansas City Chiefs or the Dallas Cowboys, your role within the 6v6 format must be executed with precision.

The current meta favors players who can read defensive spacing and exploit the "First Down Animations" and "Touchdown Celebrations" to maintain team momentum.

| Position | Difficulty | Leaderboard Impact | Key Skill |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |

| **Wide Receiver** | Medium | High | Route Running / Catching |
| **Defensive Back** | Medium | High | Zone Coverage / Intercepts |
| **Running Back** | Medium | Medium | Juking / Lane Vision |
| **Kicker** | Easy | Situational | Meter Accuracy |

**Quarterback (QB) Strategy:**
The QB is the engine of the offense. In Ranked Park, you must look past the primary receiver. Use the "Hold and Release" mechanic to adjust pass power. A "bullet" pass is necessary for tight windows, while a "lob" is better for deep routes where the receiver has beaten the Defensive Back.

**Wide Receiver (WR) Strategy:**
Success as a WR depends on separation. Do not simply run in a straight line. Use sharp cuts and changes in speed to confuse defenders. In 2026, timing your jump is critical; jumping too early allows the defender to under-cut the route for an easy interception.

<Callout type="info" title="Expert Advice">
Defensive Backs should always stay between the receiver and the end zone. Chasing a receiver from behind is a losing strategy in Ranked Park; focus on "shading" the receiver to force them toward the sidelines.
</Callout>

</Section>
