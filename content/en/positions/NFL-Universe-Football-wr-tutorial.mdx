<Section icon={Swords} title="Elite Catching Techniques & Mechanics">

Once you have mastered the basic controls, you must learn advanced catching techniques to consistently beat tight coverage. Simply running and clicking the catch button is rarely enough against high-OVR defenders. You must learn how to "attack" the ball in the air.

| Technique | Mechanics | Best Situation | Difficulty |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Standard Catch** | Jump + click before ball arrives | Open field with no defenders nearby | Easy |
| **Diving Catch** | Jump at highest point + press E | Low passes or reaching the end zone | Medium |
| **Diagonal Tech** | Creep diagonally under ball + jump early | Heavily contested jump balls | Hard |
| **Boost Tech** | Stop, jump over DB's head early | 1v1 tight coverage on deep fades | Hard |
| **Sidekick Tech** | Jump out of bounds, dive back in | Sideline passes near the boundary | Hard |

The **Diagonal Tech** is one of the most reliable methods used by competitive players. Instead of running in a straight line toward where the ball will land, slowly creep up to the landing zone at a diagonal angle. Get directly under the ball's trajectory, and jump slightly earlier than you normally would. This early jump allows you to "magnet" (or mag) the ball down at its highest peak, completely cutting off the defender's reach.

The **Boost Tech** is another highly effective maneuver. When a deep fade is thrown, stop your momentum right before the ball descends, and jump slightly to the side over the defender's head. By "earying" (jumping early), you force the defender to jump at their standard timing, which physically boosts your avatar higher into the air and gives you a massive vertical advantage.

Finally, the **Sidekick Tech** is perfect for sideline plays. Have your Quarterback purposely throw the ball slightly out of bounds. Run toward the sideline, jump out of bounds, and press the dive key (E) to catch the ball while diving back into the field of play. Because defensive backs rarely guard the out-of-bounds area, this technique results in a free, uncontested reception.

<Callout type="success" title="Timing Key">
Always remember to dive at the **highest point of your jump**. Diving too early limits your vertical reach, while diving too late causes your avatar to faceplant, resulting in an incomplete pass.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Map} title="Mastering Route Running & Separation">

To get open for these spectacular catches, you need to run clean, unpredictable routes. Running in a straight line makes you incredibly easy to cover, even for mediocre defensive players. You must use sharp cuts and directional changes to break the defender's ankles.

| Route | Execution Steps | Best Use Case | DB Reaction |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Slants / Zigzag** | Cut diagonally across the field | Quick first downs, short yardage | Gets shaken by quick direction change |
| **Post Corner** | Run to middle, cut back to corner | Deep passes, red zone scoring | Thinks you are going inside, gets burned |
| **Stop & Go** | Sprint, sudden stop, sprint deep | Burning aggressive defenders | Commits to stop, gets dusted on the go |
| **Streaks / Verts** | Run straight down the field | Deep coverage test, pure speed | Forces DB to bail deep |

To elevate your route running, implement these core strategic pillars during your matches:

  <Card title="Pillar 1: Create Separation">
    Never run in a straight line. Use routes like the **Post Corner**—where you run toward the middle of the field (post) before sharply cutting back to the corner—to leave your defender stranded in the wrong direction.

  <Card title="Pillar 2: Manage Stamina">
    Conserve your energy. Walk or jog during the initial pre-snap phase, and explode into a full sprint the moment you make your cut. This burst of speed makes your routes incredibly sharp and hard to react to.

  <Card title="Pillar 3: Avoid Cloning">

  <Card title="Pillar 4: Build QB Chemistry">

By combining these strategic pillars with the routes detailed above, you will become an unstoppable force in both casual 6v6 matches and competitive Park games.
