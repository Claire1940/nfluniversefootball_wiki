<Section icon={Map} title="Mastering WR Controls and Catching Mechanics">

To excel as an **NFL Universe Football wr**, you must have a subconscious understanding of the control scheme. Whether you are playing on PC or mobile, the timing of your inputs is the difference between a touchdown and an interception. The game utilizes a physics-based catching system where your character's orientation to the ball matters just as much as the button press.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="The Release">
    At the snap, use **WASD** and hold **Shift** to burst off the line. Avoid running straight into the defender; use a slight zig-zag to confuse their tracking.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Route Execution">
    Follow your designated route. When you reach the "break point" (the turn), let go of the sprint key momentarily to make a sharper turn, then burst again.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Calling for the Ball">
    Once you have separation, use the in-game prompt to signal the QB. Do not spam this, as it can distract the QB from reading other open teammates.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="The Catch">
    As the ball approaches, face the incoming projectile. Press **Left Click** (PC) or tap the **Catch Button** (Mobile) when the ball is within the highlighted window.
  </Step>
  <Step num="5" title="Yards After Catch (YAC)">
    Immediately after securing the ball, look for open space. Use the **E** key to dive if a defender is about to tackle you near the goal line.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="warning" title="Common Mistake">
Many beginners jump too early. Jumping (Spacebar) freezes your horizontal momentum. Only jump if the pass is high; otherwise, stay on your feet to maintain speed after the catch.
</Callout>

</Section>

<Section icon={Sparkles} title="Advanced Route Running and Field Strategy">

Effective route running is what separates a casual player from a Ranked Park veteran. In **NFL Universe Football**, defenders often play "Man-to-Man" coverage, meaning they will follow you everywhere. Your job is to use the field's geometry to make their job impossible. 

Understanding which routes work best against specific defensive alignments is key to consistent scoring. Use the table below as a playbook for your next match.

| Route Type | Best Use Case | Difficulty | Defensive Counter |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Slant** | Quick yards against aggressive blitzes or soft zone coverage. | Easy | Linebacker middle-read |
| **Streak/Fly** | Exploiting a speed advantage against a single deep safety. | Medium | Over-the-top safety help |
| **Out Route** | Creating sideline space when the middle of the field is crowded. | Medium | Cornerback sideline shade |
| **Post** | Long-distance scoring over the middle of the field. | Hard | Double coverage/Safety |
| **Comeback** | Beating defenders who are playing too far back (cushion). | Hard | Aggressive press coverage |

<Callout type="info" title="Strategy Note">
In 6v6 matches, coordinate with your fellow receivers. If one player runs a "Clear-out" streak, it often pulls the safety away, leaving the middle open for a "Drag" or "In" route.
</Callout>

</Section>
