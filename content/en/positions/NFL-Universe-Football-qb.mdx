<Section icon={Map} title="Passing Strategies and Route Reading">

Being an elite **NFL Universe Football qb** is 30% mechanics and 70% decision-making. You must learn to read the Defensive Backs (DB) before the ball even leaves your hand. In Season 6, defensive AI and player skill-based matchmaking have made the "Deep Bomb" strategy less effective against experienced opponents.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Pre-Snap Read">
    Identify if the defense is playing zone or man-to-man coverage. Look for gaps in the secondary or mismatches where a fast WR is covered by a slower defender.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The Drop-Back">
    After the snap, take a 3-step or 5-step drop. Do not sprint immediately, as this collapses the pocket and alerts defenders to your movement.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Identify the Primary Target">
    Watch your primary receiver's route. If they are jammed at the line, immediately look for your check-down option (usually a Running Back or Tight End).
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Execute the Throw">
    Time your release. For a "Bullet Pass," hold the click longer. For a "Touch Pass" over a defender's head, use a shorter hold time.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout type="success" title="Strategy Success">
Successful drives in Ranked Park often rely on "Dink and Dunk" passing—short, 5-10 yard gains that move the chains and tire out the defense.
</Callout>

</Section>
