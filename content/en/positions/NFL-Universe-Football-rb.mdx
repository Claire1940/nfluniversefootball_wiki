<Section icon={Target} title="Mastering RB Controls and Rushing Techniques">

To excel as an **NFL Universe Football rb**, you must go beyond just holding the forward key. The game features a robust control scheme that rewards precision and timing. Whether you are on PC or mobile, mastering the "Dive" and "Juke" mechanics is what separates casual players from the Ranked Park elites.

The "Sprint" mechanic is your most valuable resource, but it is finite. Constant sprinting makes your routes sloppy and leaves you vulnerable to well-timed tackles. Instead, use your sprint in bursts when you see a lane open up or when you need to turn the corner on a sweep play.

**Essential Rushing Steps:**

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Read the Defensive Front">
    Before the snap, look at the alignment of the defenders. If they are stacked in the middle, look for an outside bounce. If they are spread wide, prepare to hit the "A" or "B" gaps directly.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="The Patient Handoff">
    Don't sprint immediately upon receiving the ball. Allow your blockers a split second to engage. Moving at normal speed initially gives you better vision to spot developing holes.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Execute the Cut">
    Once you see the gap, use the Shift key (PC) or Sprint button (Mobile) to explode through it. Use your WASD keys to make slight directional adjustments rather than wide turns.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="The Finishing Dive">
    When a defender is closing in or you are near the goal line, use the 'E' key (PC) or Dive button to lung forward. This can often gain you those extra 2 yards needed for a first down.
  </Step>
</Steps>

| Action | PC Input | Mobile Input | Best Use Case |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sprint** | Shift | Sprint Button | Bursting through gaps / Outrunning DBs |
| **Dive / Tackle** | E | Dive Button | Goal line / Avoiding low tackles |
| **Juke** | A/D + Timing | Joystick Flick | Beating a defender 1-on-1 |
| **Catch / Handoff** | Left Click | Catch Button | Receiving screen passes or handoffs |

<Callout type="warning" title="Stamina Management">
Over-sprinting drains your stamina bar quickly. A tired RB is slower and more likely to fumble or fail a juke. Save at least 30% of your bar for the final breakaway.
</Callout>

</Section>
