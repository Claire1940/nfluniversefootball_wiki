<Section icon={Swords} title="Mastering Controls and Gameplay Mechanics">

The mechanical ceiling in NFL Universe Football is high. Success depends on your ability to read the field and execute precise inputs. The control scheme is optimized for PC, Mobile, and Controller, but the core physics remain consistent across all devices. Understanding the difference between a sprint-heavy playstyle and a tactical positioning playstyle is what separates rookies from Pro Bowl players.

<Callout type="warning" title="Avoid Defensive Panic">
New players often spam the **E (Dive)** key on defense. Diving too early leaves you immobile for several seconds, allowing the ball carrier to walk right past you. Only dive when you are within arm's length of the runner.
</Callout>

### Core Control Reference Table

| Action | PC Input | Mobile Input | Pro Tip |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sprint** | Left Shift | Sprint Button | Use in bursts; don't drain stamina |
| **Throw / Catch** | Left Click | Tap/Hold Button | Release at the peak of the meter |
| **Dive / Tackle** | E Key | Dive Button | Wait for the runner to commit to a lane |
| **Jump** | Spacebar | Jump Button | Time with the ball arrival, not before |
| **Handoff / Block** | X Key | Block Prompt | Essential for protecting your QB |
| **Switch View** | Mouse Scroll | Camera Icon | Keep the ball in sight at all times |

### Offensive Strategy: Passing and Rushing
When playing as the Quarterback, your primary job is to read the defense's "spacing." If the defenders are playing deep, look for short "slants" or "hitches." If they press the line, look for the "deep post." The throw meter requires a hold-and-release mechanic; holding it longer increases power but can lead to overthrows if not timed correctly.

### Defensive Strategy: Coverage and Interceptions
As a Defensive Back (DB), your goal is to stay between the receiver and the end zone. Use the "strafe" technique (moving laterally without sprinting) to mirror the receiver's movements. When the ball is in the air, wait for the indicator to turn green before pressing the catch button to secure an interception.
