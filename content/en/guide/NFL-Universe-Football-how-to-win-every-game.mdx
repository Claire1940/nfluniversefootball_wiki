<Section icon={Swords} title="Mastering Controls for Offensive Dominance">

Mastering the mechanics is the second pillar of **NFL Universe Football how to win every game**. The control scheme is deep, offering different layers of interaction for PC, mobile, and controller players. To win consistently, you must transition from "reactive" play (pressing buttons when prompted) to "proactive" play (anticipating the ball's trajectory).

Offensive success relies heavily on the Quarterback (QB) and Wide Receiver (WR) relationship. The QB must manage the throw meter by holding and releasing the left click (or tap on mobile) with precision. Releasing too early results in a weak, easily intercepted "duck," while overcharging can lead to overthrown passes that fly out of bounds.

| Action | PC Input | Mobile Input | Winning Strategy Tip |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sprint** | Shift | Sprint Button | Use in bursts to create separation |
| **Throw Pass** | Hold/Release Left Click | Tap/Hold Throw | Lead the receiver into open space |
| **Dive/Tackle** | E | Dive Button | Only dive when the runner is in reach |
| **Catch** | Left Click (Timed) | Catch Button | Face the ball and time the jump |
| **Jump** | Spacebar | Jump Button | Use at the apex of the ball's flight |

<Callout type="tip" title="Pro Passing Tip">
When playing as QB, do not throw to where the receiver is currently standing. Aim for the "green grass" ahead of their route. This forces the receiver to run into the ball, making it nearly impossible for a trailing defender to reach it in time.
</Callout>

Defensive play is often overlooked but is usually what decides close matches. A common mistake is diving too early. In NFL Universe Football, a missed dive puts your player in a recovery animation, giving the runner a free path to the end zone. Instead, "shadow" the runner by staying in front of them and only committing to the tackle when you have a clear angle.
