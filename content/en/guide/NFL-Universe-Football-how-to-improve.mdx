<Section icon={Zap} title="Offensive Controls and Passing Mechanics">

Mastering the offensive side of the ball is the most direct path regarding **NFL Universe Football how to improve**. Passing is not just about clicking a button; it involves a sophisticated "hold and release" mechanic that determines the trajectory and velocity of the football. If you release too early, the ball will float, making it an easy target for interceptions. If you hold too long, you might overshoot your receiver.

The table below breaks down the essential offensive inputs for PC and Mobile players.

| Action | PC Input | Mobile Input | Execution Tip |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Throw Pass** | Hold/Release Left Click | Tap/Hold Throw Button | Lead the receiver into open space |
| **Catch** | Left Click (Timed) | Catch Button (Prompt) | Face the ball and stand still if possible |
| **Sprint** | Shift | Sprint Toggle | Use in bursts to create separation |
| **Dive** | E | Dive Button | Use at the goal line or for low catches |
| **Handoff** | X | Handoff Prompt | Only use when the RB is in position |

<Callout type="success" title="Mechanical Advantage">
Success in the passing game is 30% power and 70% placement. Always aim for where your receiver is *going* to be, rather than where they currently stand. This forces the defender to play catch-up.
</Callout>

### Advanced Route Running
For Wide Receivers, improvement comes from "shaking" the defender. Do not run in a straight line. Use the WASD keys (or joystick) to make sharp cuts at the 5-yard or 10-yard mark. This creates a split-second window where the defender's momentum carries them away from you, giving your Quarterback a clear window to throw.
