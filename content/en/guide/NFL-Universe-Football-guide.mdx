<Section icon={Swords} title="Advanced Gameplay Mechanics">

To transition from a casual player to an elite competitor, you must master the game's core physics and mechanics [1.1]. This includes understanding passing trajectories, route running, and defensive positioning [1.1].

### Bullet Passes vs. Lob Passes
There are two primary passing trajectories in the game. To throw a bullet pass, step forward into your throw right as you release the ball. This is ideal for quick slants and tight coverage windows. To throw a lob pass, step backward or execute a fadeaway movement while releasing. This places a high arc on the ball, letting you drop passes over defensive backs on corner routes.

### Throwing with Anticipation
Do not throw the ball directly at your receiver's current position. Instead, throw with anticipation by aiming several yards in front of their running route. This allows the receiver to catch the ball in stride and maintain their momentum for extra yards after the catch.

### Utilizing Speed Pads
When playing offense, always look for the red route lines and speed pads on the field. Stepping directly onto these pads grants your receiver a massive speed boost, allowing them to instantly separate from defenders.

| Pass Type | Input Action | Ideal Scenario | Defensive Counter |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Bullet Pass** | Step forward & release Click | Quick slants, tight coverage windows | Intercepted if thrown behind receiver |
| **Lob Pass** | Step back & release Click | Deep corner routes, fade routes over DBs | Swatted if DB has superior positioning |
| **Anticipation Lead** | Aim ahead of receiver route | Slants, cross-field post routes | Cut off by zone-covering defenders [1.1] |
| **Outside Throw** | Aim toward sideline boundary | Out routes, sideline boundary catches | Out of bounds if thrown too early |

<Callout type="info" title="Jump Timing Peak">
When catching or intercepting, always jump right as the ball reaches its highest point. Jumping too early or spamming the catch button before the ball arrives will lock you into a failing animation, letting your opponent easily secure the possession.
</Callout>
