<Section icon={Target} title="How to Create and Join a Private Server">

Creating your own private space in NFL Universe Football is a straightforward process handled directly through the Roblox platform. Setting up a server allows you to generate a unique join link that bypasses public matchmaking entirely, ensuring only invited players can enter your gridiron.

<Steps>
  <Step num="1" title="Navigate to the Game Page">
    Open the Roblox launcher or website and navigate to the official NFL Universe Football game page.
  </Step>
  <Step num="2" title="Access the Servers Tab">
    Scroll down below the game description and click on the "Servers" tab to view public and private server options.
  </Step>
  <Step num="3" title="Create Private Server">
    Click the "Create Private Server" button. Depending on active developer configurations, this may require a small Robux fee or be completely free.
  </Step>
  <Step num="4" title="Configure and Share Link">
    Name your server and open the configuration menu to enable "Allow Joining." Copy the generated private server link and distribute it to your teammates or league members.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Once the server is active, players who click your link will automatically bypass the main menu matchmaking and load directly into your private field. This makes organizing team practices and community events incredibly efficient.

<Callout type="tip" title="Server Links">
You can easily share your private server link in Discord communities like the official NFL Universe Football Discord (invite code: UFB) to invite players for scrimmages.
</Callout>

As the host of an **NFL Universe Football private server**, you are granted administrative controls to manage the game flow, organize players, and maintain a clean training environment. These tools are accessed through the in-game user interface and are critical for running structured matches.

<Section icon={Swords} title="Best Practice Drills for Private Servers">

To get the most out of your private server, you should focus on drills that are difficult to practice in public games due to constant defensive pressure or uncooperative quarterbacks. By isolating these skills, you can dramatically improve your performance when you return to public matchmaking.

<CardGrid cols={2}>
  <Card title="QB Power Calibration">
    Practice throwing with precise power settings. Calibrate your power to **80% to 85%** for short, quick routes, and bump it up to **90%** for medium passes over the middle.
  </Card>
  <Card title="DB User-Lurking">
    Have a friend play quarterback while you practice staying directly in front of the wide receiver. Work on timing your jump to secure interceptions consistently.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Route Running & Pads">
    Practice running clean route trees (like posts, corners, and slants). Utilize the wide receiver pads on the field to master the speed boost mechanic.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Sidetech & Glitches">
    Master advanced movement mechanics like sidetech without the distraction of public players trying to tackle you.
  </Card>
</CardGrid>

Calibrating your throwing power is the difference between a completion and an interception. Use the following guidelines during your private server passing drills to find the perfect power for every situation:

| Distance | Power Level | Target Area |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Short Pass** | 80% - 85% | Throw slightly above receiver head |
| **Medium Pass** | 85% - 90% | Lead the receiver to the outside |
| **Deep Pass / Bomb** | 95% - 100% | High arc, target open field space |

<Callout type="success" title="Speed Boost Tip">
Always step on the wide receiver pads before starting your route. The pad grants a massive speed boost that allows you to burn defensive backs on deep routes.
</Callout>
